CrowdVoxel-Net: Voxelized geometry and transformer-based multimodal fusion for activity recognition and anomaly detection in UAV-based crowded scenes

A geometry-aware multimodal framework that fuses voxelized orthogonal depth projections, self-supervised semantics, dense point tracking, and keypoint correspondences to recognize human activities and detect anomalies from aerial drone imagery.

01 · Abstract

Reasoning about geometry, motion, and semantics from the sky.

Recognizing human activities and detecting anomalous behaviors in crowded scenes from unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) imagery remains a challenging problem due to severe occlusions, scale compression, viewpoint distortion, and weak appearance cues, which limit the effectiveness of conventional ground-based and 2D appearance-driven methods.

To address these challenges, this paper proposes CrowdVoxel-Net, a geometry-aware multimodal framework for UAV-based activity recognition and anomaly detection. A novel aerial silhouette extraction network — SA-Net — enables reliable human foreground isolation under complex drone viewpoints. Pseudo-3D human structure is reconstructed via monocular depth estimation and encoded using voxelized orthogonal depth projections. Long-range spatiotemporal motion is modeled with CoTracker, and domain-invariant semantics are extracted with a Vision Transformer–based DINO backbone. Local structural cues are strengthened with a compact SuperPoint + LightGlue descriptor and fast cross-frame matching module. These complementary geometric, semantic, spatial, and temporal representations are fused with a Transformer-based classifier for joint activity recognition and anomaly detection.

Key contributions

  • SA-Net — a hierarchical encoder–decoder segmentation model with a global context bottleneck and boundary-conserving decoder, tailored to aerial silhouette extraction under scale compression and viewpoint distortion.
  • Voxelized orthogonal depth projections — monocular depth is lifted to a pseudo-3D voxel grid and projected across front, top, side, isometric, cabinet, military, and cavalier views to capture view-consistent volumetric cues.
  • Motion + correspondence stack — CoTracker delivers long-range dense trajectories; SuperPoint + LightGlue provides sparse, geometry-aware cross-frame matches confined to human regions.
  • Transformer fusion — a positional-encoded encoder–decoder produces a joint embedding used for activity classification and anomaly scoring.

Takeaways

  • Voxelized orthogonal projections contribute the largest ablation drop, confirming that explicit 3D spatial reasoning is critical under aerial views.
  • DINO semantics and dense motion tracking provide complementary gains, especially on complex fine-grained action classes.
  • SuperPoint + LightGlue correspondences add moderate but consistent stability to geometric alignment across frames.
  • Fusion delivers a new state-of-the-art on ShanghaiTech, Drone Action, and both UAV-Human protocols.
